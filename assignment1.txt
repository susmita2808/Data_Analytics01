1. What do you mean by cells in an excel sheet?

A cell is the intersection of a row and a column.
 

2. How can you restrict someone from copying a cell from your worksheet?

To protect my worksheet from getting copied, I need to go into Menu bar >Review > Protect sheet > Password.
By entering password,I can secure my worksheet from getting copied by others.


3. How to move or copy the worksheet into another workbook?

On the Edit menu, click Sheet > Move or Copy Sheet. 
On the To book menu, click the workbook that I want to copy the sheet to.


4. Which key is used as a shortcut for opening a new window document?

Ctrl+N


5. What are the things that we can notice after opening the Excel interface?

The Excel Starter startup screen appears, and a blank spreadsheet is displayed. 
In Excel Starter, a spreadsheet is called a worksheet, here we can see Title bar, Formula Bar,Row Headings, Column Headings, cell, 
Quick Access Tool bars, Name Box, Status Bar, Sheet Tabs.



6. When to use a relative cell reference in excel?
This is the most widely used type of cell reference in formulas in Excel. This reference changes when the formula is copied
to any other cell or any other worksheet. 
Relative references are especially convenient whenever I need to repeat the same calculation across multiple rows or columns.
